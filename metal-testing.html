Metallurgical Analysis

Macrostructure Analysis is performed to access metal welding quality, casting formation, or identify natural imperfection which is normally found in the form of impurity or voids.

Microstructure analysis is conducted to examine internal structure of metal which has gone through polishing and etching processes. Through actual photo analysis varying from 25 to 1000 magnification, engineers can obtain preliminary production information.

Replica technique is the duplication of a surface structure of the inspected metal onto a special film. This is a non-destructive test as the study sample is untouched. This technique is best for large scale materials which cannot easily be transported such as boilers and oil refinery machinery.

Indentation hardness test measures hardness in Vickers and Rockwell.

Corrosion tests, such as Pitting corrosion and intergranular corrosion, are normally performed on stainless steel to measures its vulnerability to corrosion in order to access its quality before use.

  • Macro/Micro Structure Analysis of Material and Weld Specimen
  • Hardness Test
  • Sulfur Prints
  • Replica Technique
  • Pitting Corrosion Test
  • Intergranular Corrosion test
  • Metal Flow/ Fiber Flow
  • Yellow Mount Weld
